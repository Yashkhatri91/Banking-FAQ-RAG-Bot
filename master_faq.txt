FAB Mobile
- From the Home screen, tap the menu on the top right corner. Tap 'Edit Profile' and select the 'Personal Details' tab to to edit your information such as mobile number, email address and home address
Online Banking
- Select 'Services', go to 'Other Services' and click on 'Update Mobile Number'
How do I update my Passport/ Visa details in my bank records?
You can update your Passport or Visa information through:
FAB Mobile
-From the Home screen, tap the menu on the top right corner. Go to the 'Edit Profile' option and select the 'Documents' tab. Tap 'Add a new document' and choose 'Passport' from the menu to upload your new document. You can do the same for Visa
Online Banking
- Click on your Profile name and select 'My Documents'. From here, select 'Add New Document', choose 'Passport' from the drop down menu and upload the document. You can do the same for the Visa

FAB website
- Use our IBAN Generator tool

-Find your IBAN number under your account details on the Home Page

=== Emirates NBD FAQ ===

Q: Choose from our range of accounts
A: We have a range of current and savings accounts you can choose from based on your needs and financial goals. It may make sense to open more than one account - a checking account and a savings account - so you have access to cash for your daily expenses and enjoy the best interest rates in the market too. If you have children under 18, it's a good idea to open accounts for minors.   

We'll help you open your account the same day if you make sure to provide all the documents required when submitting your application. 
Q: where ccan i check best interest rates?
A: You can browse through our range of savings accounts where you'll find more information on interest rates.
Q: when will i recceive my debit card and ccheque book?
A: If youвҖҷve applied for a debit card and cheque book, you'll receive them within 3 to 5 working days of your account opening.  
Q: I could not open my first account online, what should i do?
A: You might have to submit additional details. You should receive a call from us to take your request forward. If you donвҖҷt, please call us on +971 600 54 0000.
Q: how soon caan i operate my first account?
A: If youвҖҷre a new customer, you can start operating your account as soon as you receive your debit card. Once you activate your card, you can use it for purchases and withdraw cash at ATMs, deposit cash directly to your account through our cash deposit machines, and register for Online Banking and Mobile Banking (ENBD X).
Q: whats is a credit card?
A: A credit card is a financial tool issued by banks that allows you to borrow money up to a predetermined limit to make purchases or withdraw cash. The borrowed amount must be repaid by the due date, either in full or through instalments, with interest applicable on outstanding balances. Credit cards also offer various benefits such as rewards, cashback, and travel perks.
Q: whats is a cashback credit card?
A: A cashback credit card rewards you with a percentage of your spending in the form of cash rebates. For example, if a card offers 5% cashback on groceries, you receive о«ҹ 5 back for every о«ҹ 100 spent. Cashback cards in the UAE often have specific categories like fuel, dining, or online shopping where higher cashback rates apply
Q: What is balance trasnfer in Credit card?
A: A balance transfer lets you move credit card debt to a lower-interest card for easier repayment. Click here for more information.
Q: How can I get a Credit card in UAE?
A: We have a range of Credit Cards with different rewards and benefits for you to choose from. All Emirates NBD Credit Cardholders can get up to five supplementary Credit Cards for dependents. 

To apply for a Credit Card, you must be at least 21 years of age and meet the minimum income requirement for the card.

If you're self-employed, you'll need to have an average bank balance of at least о«ҹ 50,000 over the last 3 months. In addition, you may need to open a Fixed Deposit with Emirates NBD which holds the minimum deposit amount required for the card youвҖҷre applying for. 
Q: how much can i borrow on personal loan?
A: Most banks will prescribe a limit on your personal loan based on your income and ability to repay. However, itвҖҷs always advisable to borrow an amount that you will be able to safely repay without getting into debt you canвҖҷt manage
Q:should i go for fater processing or lower interest rate?
A: A low EMI could actually lead to a longer payment period and could prove more expensive as you will pay more interest. Moreover, fast processing may force you to take a hasty decision without complete information, so be careful and ask all the questions you need to before you sign up.
Q: Waht is the tenure for ENBD personal loan?
A: The maximum tenure for an Emirates NBD personal loan is 48 months. UAE Nationals in the Ministry of Defense can extend their loan tenure up to 60 months.

=== Emirates NBD Account Opening - Manual ===

Q: How do I open an account at Emirates NBD?
A: You can open an Emirates NBD account in three ways. First, visit any Emirates NBD branch with your Emirates ID and supporting documents. Second, apply through the ENBD X mobile app by downloading it and following the account opening steps. Third, apply online at emiratesnbd.com. New customers can open an account the same day if all required documents are provided.

Q: What documents do I need to open an Emirates NBD account?
A: To open an Emirates NBD account you need your Emirates ID, a valid passport with residence visa for expats, and proof of address such as a utility bill or tenancy contract. Salaried customers may also need a salary certificate or bank statement.

Q: What is the minimum balance for an Emirates NBD account?
A: The minimum monthly average balance for Emirates NBD Classic package is AED 3,000. If this balance is not maintained a fall below fee of AED 26.25 per month is charged. UAE Nationals and salary transfer customers are exempt from this fee.

Q: How long does it take to open an Emirates NBD account?
A: Emirates NBD can open your account the same day if you provide all required documents at the time of application. Your debit card and cheque book will be delivered within 3 to 5 working days after account opening.

Q: Can I open an Emirates NBD account online or through the app?
A: Yes. You can start the account opening process through the ENBD X mobile app or online at emiratesnbd.com. You will need to verify your Emirates ID and complete a video KYC process. The account can be operational within 1 to 2 business days.

Q: differene between savings and current account?
A: Current and savings accounts serve different financial purposes:

Current Accounts are ideal for your day-to-day banking transactions, such as local and international money transfers, bill payments, including facilities like cheque books and overdrafts.
Savings Accounts are designed for saving money over time and usually offer interest on deposits. They may have lower minimum balance requirements and encourage regular savings habits.

Q: how to open a ENBD bank account?
A: Opening a UAE bank account in the UAE is straightforward with Emirates NBD. You just need a few documents to open your account online:

For UAE Residents:
Emirates ID
Valid passport
Proof of address (e.g., utility bill or tenancy contract)
Salary transfer letter or recent payslips (for salary accounts)
For Non-Residents:
Valid passport with visa page
Proof of address
Reference letter from your home country's bank
Recent bank statements
Haven't used your account for over 12 months?
If you have a current or savings account and have not used it for more than 12 months, your account will be classified as inactive. If you have not used your account and have had no contact with the bank for more than 3 years, your account will be classified as dormant. This holds true for all accounts under your name.

Once your account is classified as inactive, you'll still be able to make debit and credit transactions, but there may be delays with clearance of cheques and transferring funds. Once your account becomes dormant, you canвҖҷt do any transactions via online or mobile banking or via ATMs.
